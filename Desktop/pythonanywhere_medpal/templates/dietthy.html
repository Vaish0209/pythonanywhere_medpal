Diet for Hypothyroidism

1. Eggs
  • Whole eggs are best, as much of their iodine and selenium are found in the yolk, while the whites are full of protein
2. Nuts and legumes
  • Nuts and legumes are high in selenium, which can affect the activation of thyroid hormones.
  • Brazil nuts, in particular, contain exceptionally high levels of selenium
3. Tuna & sardiness
  • Just below Brazil nuts on the list of foods naturally high in selenium are tuna and sardines.
  • Sardines are high in selenium to support thyroid function. Additionally, sardines are rich in omega-3 fatty acids, which help lower inflammation and enhance immunity.




4. Oysters
  • Zinc is also an important nutrient in managing thyroid conditions.
  • Zinc helps the body produce thyroid hormones [and while] zinc deficiencies are rare, people with hypothyroidism may still want to eat foods rich in zinc, like oysters and other shellfish.
5. Seafood
  • Fish, shrimp, and seaweed are great sources of iodine. You need iodine for a healthy thyroid.
  • But avoid large amounts of iodine-rich choices like kelp.
6. Dairy products
  • Dairy products are some of the best sources of bioavailable iodine.
  • In addition to being a good source of calcium and good fats, it is also a rich source of iodine.

What to avoid?

1. Soy-based foods
  • In rare cases, some of the chemicals found in soy products like soy milk or edamame could hurt your thyroid’s ability to make hormones, but only if you don't get enough iodine and eat large amounts.
  • You don’t have to avoid soy-based foods entirely, but it’s smart to eat them in moderation and wait several hours after taking your medication to enjoy these foods.
2. Cruciferous and leafy green vegetables
  • Try to avoid chowing down excessively on cabbage, broccoli, kale, Brussels sprouts, cauliflower, and spinach.These veggies tend to be higher in goitrogens which might be an issue for people who have an iodine deficiency.
  • However, their effect on thyroid function can typically be further reduced by cooking these foods thoroughly before eating them, as opposed to eating them raw or undercooked.
3. Millet
  • Millet has been shown to reduce thyroid function, even when people don’t eat it excessively or aren’t already deficient in other nutrients, like iodine.
4. Caffeinated and alcoholic beverages
  • Coffee, green tea, and alcohol can irritate your thyroid gland, so it’s best to consume these in moderation rather than all day, every day.
  • Alcohol can suppress your thyroid function, decreasing the amount of thyroid hormones, especially with chronic use.
5. Gluten
  • Gluten is a protein found in wheat, rye, and barley. Unless you've been diagnosed with celiac disease, it probably won’t affect your thyroid.
  • Gluten can damage the small intestines of people with celiac disease. Therefore, it is important to limit or eliminate gluten if you have also been diagnosed with celiac.

Diet for Hyperthyroidism

1. Leafy greens
  • Leafy greens such as spinach and kale are an important source of calcium.
  • This can help people with hyperthyroidism because excess thyroid hormone production can lead to breakdown of bones and an increased risk of osteoporosis.
2. Fish rich in omega-3 fatty acids
  • Fatty fish like salmon are an important source of vitamin D, which helps improve your body’s calcium absorption.
  • Research indicates that omega-3 fatty acids contribute to improved liver function, which can be negatively impacted by thyroid dysregulation.
3. Brazil nuts
  • Selenium has been shown to have possible benefits in autoimmune thyroid diseases like Graves’ disease.
  • It is found in brazil nuts in high concentrations; just two to three brazil nuts can provide the recommended 200 mcg of selenium per day.







4. Lean meats and liver
  • These iron rich foods help increase your iron levels.
  • Zinc helps the body produce thyroid hormones [and while] zinc deficiencies are rare, people with hypothyroidism may still want to eat foods rich in zinc, like oysters and other shellfish.
5. Olive oil
  • Olive oil can reduce inflammation in the body and improve the thyroid function.
6. Fermented foods
  • Foods such as kimchi, kombucha, kefir, and sauerkraut contain probiotics. These help enhance gut health and may help regulate thyroid function.

What to avoid?

1. Caffeine
  • Too much caffeine can cause insomnia, anxiety, restlessness, and heart palpitations. While caffeine may not directly affect the thyroid, the results of excessive intake are the same as some hyperthyroidism symptoms.
  • Therefore, it is best to avoid caffeinated drinks
2. Seaweed
  • Although iodine supplements and consumption may be beneficial to people with hypothyroidism, a low-iodine diet is recommended because too much iodine can worsen hyperthyroidism.
  • Replacing iodized table salt with Himalayan or sea salt can also help limit iodine consumption.
3. Egg yolks
  • Eggs yolks are rich in iodine. Hence, not advisable
4. Milk or dairy products
  • It includes butter, cheese, ice cream, milk chocolate, and yogurt.
5. Gluten
  • Gluten is a protein found in wheat, rye, and barley.
  • Ggluten can cause inflammation, which damages thyroid health.
